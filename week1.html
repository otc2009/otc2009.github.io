As We May Think
By Vannevar Bush
Bush argues that we should not be building tools to discover anymore, but rather tools to help us organize and retrieve info at higher levels, considering the amount of info and data we intake. We are at a point of information overload, and any more discovery is futile without proper equipment to take it in.

For 1945, Bush was highly intuitive to predict this issue. The amount of information available is not healthy for what our brains and tools can even manage today, so for him to anticipate this problem getting worse is quite impressive. I found the essay to be inspiring because it reminded me that ideas of improving how we may think and share info have a very long history. He shows how imagination can shape the future of technology because it reminds us not to assume that bettertech alone can solve most human problems. We need to not think so linearly.
