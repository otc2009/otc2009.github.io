The Medium is the Message
By Marshall McLuhan
McLuhan's main message within this reading is that the medium of which we consume our media is what shapes society, and the content it contains comes second. Technology that can share and divide information instantaneously perpepuates individualism and isolation. As technology has only improved, we've had to engage less with what we consume. Newspapers would require us to read and search for what we want to read, while social media blasts videos meticulously given to us by some complicated algorithm atuned to our needs. It's scary to think of where we will be in ten years with how we choose to communicate.

Personally, reading this only heightens certain fears I have about our developing technology. Generations may be raised to barely think for themselves, consuming media without any critical thinking whatsoever. We are slowly being zombified. We need to start wondering what our technology does to us quietly while we're busy using it.
