Long Live the Web
By Tim Berners-Lee
Long Live the Web does a good job at trying to emphasize the importance of keeping the web as accessible and free from centralized control as much as possible. Just as our right to free speech stands, this should obviously extend to the tool that everyone uses to communicate and share information in our day and age.

I think what caught me off guard was when the article named off legislation passed by governments in the past that limit our ability to truly be free when surfing the internet. I had not realized how many rules have been put on our access to free info. I believe as time goes on, this problem of limitation will only get worse, as companies will only monopolize more and grow control over what we can see/not see.

I also believe that too much internet freedom can lead to problems. Giving anyone the ability to post and influence others can be risky if not monitored to some capacity. This could help protect the privacy and safety of consumers if small intervention was made with coorperations and governments.
