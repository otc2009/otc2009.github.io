The Mozilla Manifesto Addendum
I believe what the Mozilla manifesto declares has all the right intentions, and I agree that the internet should serve to be our greatest tool without space for discrimination and hatred. However, I find it to be highly unrealistic. The internet gives people the ability to say and post whatever they want with no consequences. Everyone is able to talk to one another without knowing anybody at all.

You cannot give people complete freedom over their use of the internet and not expect hatred to spew out. There are a lot of scary people out there roaming the web. As optimistic as it is to strive for tranquility within the internet, it is not realistic.

However, I think the issues that arise with the internet deserve our time and attention. There have to be ways to better manage the inputs and outputs of information, as well as better handling of how we communicate with one another.
